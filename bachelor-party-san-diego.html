BACHELOR PARTY
SAN DIEGO

By SDBachParty.com Team·Updated April 2025·8 min read

San Diego is a world-class bachelor party destination. Craft beer capital of America, Torrey Pines golf, boat parties on the bay, Gaslamp nightlife, surf, Tijuana day trips, and weather that almost never fails — the city delivers for groups at every budget and every vibe. Here's everything you need to plan a bachelor party weekend the groom will never forget.

Top picks

BEST BACHELOR PARTY IDEAS IN SAN DIEGO

01
Private Boat Charter on San Diego Bay
A 75-foot catamaran with a full bar, waterslide, and the Coronado Bridge as your backdrop. Nothing in San Diego beats a private yacht charter for a group of guys — active, social, and genuinely spectacular. Accommodates up to 100 guests.
Most Popular
02
North Park Craft Brewery Tour
San Diego has 150+ craft breweries — the most per capita of any city in America. North Park's 30th Street corridor has Modern Times, Mike Hess, Thorn Street, and more within walking distance. Tasting flights at 4-5 stops makes a perfect afternoon.
Craft Beer
03
Golf at Torrey Pines
One of the most spectacular public golf courses in America — ocean views on every hole and the site of multiple US Opens. Book tee times weeks in advance. The South Course is the more challenging and impressive of the two.
Golf
04
Gaslamp Quarter Bar Crawl
16 blocks of bars and clubs in a beautifully preserved Victorian district downtown. Omnia nightclub, Bang Bang, FLUXX, and dozens of bars between them — the Gaslamp is San Diego's most reliable nightlife destination for a group.
Nightlife
05
Day Trip to Tijuana
30 minutes from downtown — park on the US side and walk across. Incredible tacos, craft breweries (Tijuana has a surprisingly great beer scene), and an experience completely unlike anything in the US. Back in San Diego for dinner. Bring your passport.
Day Trip
06
Surf Lessons at La Jolla Shores
Group surf lessons at La Jolla Shores — the calmest and most beginner-friendly beach in San Diego. Most guys in the group will be standing up within an hour. Active, competitive, and a genuinely fun group activity that's different from everything else on the list.
Active
PLANNING TIPS FOR SAN DIEGO BACHELOR
  • Book Torrey Pines tee times the moment they become available — the South Course fills up fast, especially weekend mornings.
  • The boat charter is the anchor activity — book it first and plan the rest of the weekend around it.
  • Rent a group house in Pacific Beach for the ideal bachelor party base — beach access, outdoor space, and central location.
  • For the Tijuana day trip, bring cash (USD works fine) and your passport. Stay on Avenida Revolucion and the craft beer district for the best experience.
  • The Gaslamp dress code at clubs is enforced on weekends — no shorts, no athletic wear, collared shirts appreciated.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is the best bachelor party activity in San Diego?
A private boat charter on San Diego Bay is consistently the most popular bachelor party activity — up to 100 guests, full bar, waterslide, and the bay at sunset. Golf at Torrey Pines and a North Park brewery tour are close seconds depending on the group's interests.
How much does a San Diego bachelor party cost?
A typical San Diego bachelor party weekend costs $300-$600 per person including accommodation, activities, and nightlife. Golf at Torrey Pines runs $200-$250 per person. A private boat charter splits well across large groups — 20 guys at $975/hour for 3 hours is roughly $150 per person.
Is San Diego a good bachelor party destination?
San Diego is one of the best bachelor party cities in America — craft beer, world-class golf, boat parties, Gaslamp nightlife, surf, beaches, and a day trip to Tijuana all in one weekend. The weather is almost always cooperative and the city is easy to navigate.
What neighborhood should we stay in for a bachelor party in San Diego?
Pacific Beach is the ideal neighborhood for a bachelor party group house — beach access, a lively bar scene on Garnet Avenue, and central location. Mission Beach is similar. Stay downtown near the Gaslamp if nightclubs are the priority.
